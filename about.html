Who am I?

I am a molecular biologist with interdisciplinary expertise spanning single-cell omics, spatial technologies, bioinformatics, and advanced fluorescence imaging. I bring a hybrid profile with both wet-lab and computational experience.


During my Ph.D. at the University of Cambridge, I characterized the transcriptomic and chromatin landscape of mouse embryonic development using single-cell RNA-seq and ATAC-seq technologies. My work (Pijuan-Sala et al., Nature, 2019; Pijuan-Sala et al., Nature Cell Biology, 2020) contributed to building comprehensive single-cell atlases, and was primarily computational, equipping me with bioinformatics and data analysis skills. In my postdoc at EMBL, I transitioned to a more experimental role, developing and implementing spatial technologies in Drosophila embryos. I also developed custom image analysis pipelines to investigate how chromatin states unfold during development.


Core expertise:

  • Single-cell omics and spatial technologies
  • Image analysis and bioinformatics
  • Technology development
  • Molecular & developmental biology

What drives me: coming up with new strategies to tackle biological problems - from setting up a new experimental method to developing computational workflows. I’m drawn to the engineering aspect of biology: thinking creatively, troubleshooting, and bridging tools across disciplines.


I'm excited by opportunities where technical curiosity, computational thinking, and experimental intuition come together to push the boundaries of what we can measure and understand in biology.
